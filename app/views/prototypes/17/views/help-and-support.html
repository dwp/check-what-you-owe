Help and support

We aim to help you set up a sustainable way to repay the money you owe to the Department for Work and Pensions. This information is designed to support you if you need help with your debt.

If you think we’ve made a mistake

If there are debts here that you don’t recognise or that you think might have been added to your account by mistake, contact Debt Management immediately.

Telephone: 0800 916 0614
Textphone: 0345 604 6697
Monday to Friday 8am to 7.30pm
Saturday 9am to 4pm

The Department for Work and Pensions collects money for a range of different teams and organisations.

If you’d like to discuss the details of any specific loan or overpayment, you’ll need to talk to the people who made it. You can find their contact details above or on the contact page.

If you’re struggling to pay

Contact Debt Management immediately if you think you might miss a payment. You can call us to ask for a payment holiday, change the amount you pay each month or to ask for help and advice.

Telephone: 0800 916 0614
Textphone: 0345 604 6697
Monday to Friday 8am to 7.30pm
Saturday 9am to 4pm

If you’d like independent advice

Citizens Advice

For help and advice on dealing with debt, budgeting and more, contact Citizens Advice.

They are a network of charities who can help you stay on top of your finances. You can get help over the phone, online, or in person.

StepChange

You can also contact StepChange for expert advice on debt. They are a charity who can help you look at your debt and set up a solution. You can get help by letter, email, phone, or online, and their online chat service is open 24 hours a day.
